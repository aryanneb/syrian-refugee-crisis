About

What is the problem?.

Millions of people are affected by an ongoing war inside of Syria. This leads to millions of innocent citizens losing their basic human rights. These people all need food, water and shelter but this can be extremely expensive.

Many people believe that to solve the Syrian refugee crisis we need to welcome them into our country. Although a good idea at first, integration into our society will be difficult for some as they are not used to our laws, our cultural norms and our views. For example, in Syria any LGBT action is punished with death, and if these refugees who believe in that, come to Canada or Europe and see that happening, their reaction could lead to severe problems. Because of this other people believe that we should donate to organizations who help out people in Syria. For example Canada spent 385$ million dollars to take in 20,000. With that kind of money you could help out 130,000 refugees in Jordan because it costs only $3,000 for food and water and shelter in Jordan. Sure by coming over here they get more opportunities but is supporting 20,000 really better than supporting 130,000.

Helping the refugees won't be cheap and the only way we can help as many refugees out is by donating instead of resettling. Currently that is the only viable option because resettling costs almost 10 times as much as helping out organizations that are there.

Timeline of the War

This is just a simple timeline showing what happened over the course of this war. Keep in mind that not all details are present, just the major details.

Timeline

March 28, 2011

Protest Crackdown

60 Dead So Far

The protests accross the country have been continuing for several days and security forces have reportedly claimed up to 60 lives so far.

July 19, 2012

Conflict speards

Aleppo

The conflict that started not too long before this, has now spread to one of the main cities in the country, Aleppo.

March 2013 - August 2013

Gas Attacks

Asaad blamed for attacks.

Deadly gas attacked are released and soldiers in Khan al-Assal and hundreds of rebels in Damascus are all killed and many more are injured.

September 23, 2014

United States Intervention

Airstrikes

The United States of America launches airstrikes against the "Islamic State" (IS) as they are seen as a threat to Americans.

September 30, 2015

Russian Intervention

Bombing

After continually providing support to the Asaad government, Russia actively enters conflict. They bomb rebel bases including hospitals and civillian homes. The war now shifts in favor for the Asaad government.

July 7, 2017

Ceasefire

America and Trump

The Trump administration and Russian president Vladimir Putin agree to a limited ceasefire in three provinces in southwest Syria. Although good news, many people wonder if this will last for a long time.
